Israel’s Colocation Market: Technical Evaluation and Why MedOne Leads

A focused technical review of Israel’s colocation landscape and why MedOne stands out for resilience, interconnection, and sovereignty.

Israel’s data center and colocation market is no longer a niche infrastructure segment. It has become one of the most strategically important layers in the country’s digital economy. The rapid growth of cloud adoption, AI workloads, fintech platforms, cybersecurity vendors and government digitization has turned colocation from hosting space into a mission critical anchor for national resilience.

Demand continues to rise at a steady pace. Enterprises need higher power density, more interconnect options, and stronger compliance controls. At the same time, the physical risk profile in Israel is unique: geopolitical instability, grid continuity challenges, and a real need for underground, fortified infrastructure. In this context, choosing a colocation provider is not a procurement decision. It is a long term risk, resilience and performance commitment.

This article provides a technical review of the Israeli colocation landscape, analyzes key players, and explains why MedOne stands out as the strongest option for organizations looking for reliable, scalable and sovereign-compliant data center infrastructure.

The Technical Landscape: What Matters in Israel

When evaluating colocation providers in Israel, five engineering driven criteria determine the real quality of a platform.

Resilience and Physical Protection

Underground construction, Tier-III+ design, N+N power, independent cooling paths and proven history through real world disruptions.

Network and Interconnection Depth

Carrier neutrality, access to all ISPs, submarine cables, cloud on ramps and low latency paths across Israel and abroad.

Scalability for Modern Loads

Availability of megawatt scale expansion, high density power for AI and HPC racks at 40–50kW, reliable cooling and strong electrical design.

Security, Compliance and Sovereignty

ISO 27001, ISO 27017, ISO 22301, SOC 2, GDPR alignment, physical access controls, 24x7 security and guaranteed data residency.

Service Ecosystem

Interconnection, IaaS, DRaaS, backup, cloud connectivity, migration support, remote hands and GPU services.

The Main Players in Israel

Below is a neutral analysis of the key operators active in Israel’s colocation market. All have strengths. Only one has the complete package.

MedOne

Israel’s largest and most mature colocation and hybrid cloud platform. The company operates underground, Tier-III+ engineered facilities across the country backed by a 99.999% availability SLA and a long operational history serving government ministries, financial institutions, defense organizations, hyperscalers and global tech companies. MedOne is also Israel’s exclusive national interconnection hub, the only environment where all carriers, submarine cables, cloud regions, ISPs, and fiber networks converge.

Bynet Data Centers

Strong underground infrastructure with 24x7 operations. Well integrated with the RAD-Bynet telecom ecosystem. Less carrier-neutral, more telco-centric in design and interconnection.

Bezeq International

Multiple facilities in central Israel with strong connectivity due to Bezeq’s backbone. Good for existing Bezeq customers, but tied heavily to the operator’s own network stack and not a neutral interconnection environment.

SDS

One of the fastest growing high capacity operators focusing on high density modern campuses. Strong for very large deployments but with a shorter operational history and a limited enterprise ecosystem compared to MedOne.

EdgeConneX and Global Technical Realty

Global players building hyperscale ready campuses near the national fiber hub. Excellent for cloud providers or large tenants but enterprise services and interconnection ecosystems are still maturing.

Triple C

Tier IV branded facility in Petach Tikva focused on SMB and mid market cloud and hosting. Smaller scale and less interconnection depth.

Technical Comparison

Resilience and underground engineering are critical in Israel. MedOne and Bynet are the two operators with a true underground, bunker grade design. MedOne goes further with large scale underground campuses, redundant power paths and real world resilience proven over decades of geopolitical incidents. SDS and hyperscale players offer modern designs but mainly above ground. Bezeq International and Triple C emphasize redundancy but not national level fortification.

Power, Cooling, Density

AI and HPC workloads require 30–50kW per rack, advanced cooling and high megawatt availability. MedOne supports high density deployments, liquid cooling options and continuous expansion in MW capacity. SDS and hyperscale entrants also target high density but mainly for a few large tenants.

Network and Interconnection

MedOne is the only true neutral hub where all submarine cables land or connect, all Israeli carriers maintain presence, ISPs and cloud providers interconnect, multiple cloud regions use private connectivity, and enterprises can build low latency east west topologies. Bynet and Bezeq offer strong connectivity but are tied to their parent networks. Hyperscale operators focus on private cloud connections not broad enterprise ecosystems.
